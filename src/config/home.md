# Description

C4DM Studios is a high-end recording studio, performance space and research facility specialising in sound and multimodal production.
This facility primarily serves to support research and teaching activities relating to the fields of audio engineering, digital signal processing, human-computer interaction, music informatics, music perception and cognition, augmented instruments, immersive technologies, AI & music, and performance studies.
As well, we work closely alongside external arts practitioners, offering production support for both live and studio projects, whilst also affording the curation of explorative and experimental practices.

C4DM Studios operates from within the School of Electronic Engineering and Computer Science (EECS) at Queen Mary University of London.
Located on the ground floor of the Electronic Engineering building, C4DM Studios is composed of three spaces: the Performance Lab (G2B), the Control Room (G2C), and the Live Room (G4).

# Affiliations

Established by the [Centre for Digital Music (C4DM)](https://c4dm.eecs.qmul.ac.uk/) and [Cognitive Science Research Group(CogSci)](https://cogsci.eecs.qmul.ac.uk/), C4DM Studios are now also working in collaboration with the [Centre for Multimodal AI (CMAI)](https://www.seresearch.qmul.ac.uk/cmai/).
We are currently a core facility supporting the Centres for Doctoral Training in [Media & Arts Technology (MAT)](https://mat.qmul.ac.uk/) and [AI & Music (AIM)](https://www.aim.qmul.ac.uk/), as well as the [MSc Sound & Music Computing](https://www.qmul.ac.uk/postgraduate/taught/coursefinder/courses/sound-and-music-computing-msc/) course.
